Zegendia’s legacy line, *“we turn customers into fans,”* still matters, but today it needs a stronger interpretation.

It is not only about delivering a prize. It is about designing an experience that makes people feel there is a relationship, not just a transaction.

## The common mistake

Many loyalty programs still depend on a generic catalog, broad campaigns, and the same logic for every participant. The outcome is predictable:

- low activation
- weak redemption
- little brand memory

## What actually works

The strongest programs usually combine three layers:

1. A clearly defined behavior to motivate.
2. A reward with strong perceived value.
3. A redemption experience that does not break trust.

Even a small reward can have more impact than an expensive one if it appears at the right moment, with the right context, for the right audience.

## The LATAM opportunity

Across Latin America, many companies want to invest in loyalty but slow down when operations become messy: fragmented providers, difficult delivery, poor visibility, or catalogs that do not fit each country.

That is why the new Zegendia ecosystem is organized into:

- `Zegendia Core` for strategy and experience design.
- `Oh My Rewards` for multi-country reward fulfillment.
- `PuntosPlus` for configurable programs that launch without rebuilding everything.

## The key idea

Loyalty is not earned just because a program exists. It is earned when the brand understands the behavior it wants to recognize and has the operational muscle to complete the reward cycle well.
