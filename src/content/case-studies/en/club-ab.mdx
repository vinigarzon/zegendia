## The challenge

Club AB+ responds to a very common Latin American business reality: merchants, distributors, and stores with recurring customers, but without a digital structure that turns purchases into relationship, data, and rewards.

The challenge was not simply to create a card. It was to give customers a clear reason to come back while giving the commercial network a more measurable way to communicate benefits and capture participation.

## The solution

Zegendia structured Club AB+ as a multi-store loyalty program where users can register for free, receive a virtual card, earn points from purchases, and redeem value within the ecosystem.

- Digital registration for new members.
- Virtual card to identify each participant.
- Point accrual from purchases at participating stores.
- Redemption for rewards, benefits, or money depending on program rules.
- Clear communication around allied brands and merchants.

## Why it matters

Club AB+ supports a key part of Zegendia's new positioning: loyalty does not belong only to banks, airlines, or large retailers.

It can also work for local commercial networks, distributors, specialized stores, regional chains, and buyer communities that need a simple, credible experience with visible benefits.

## What it validates

This case validates a structure PuntosPlus can replicate in other contexts:

- multi-location programs
- benefits for frequent customers
- incentives for repeat purchases
- digital participant identity
- point redemption for real value
- operations adaptable by merchant, campaign, or audience

In short, Club AB+ shows that a network of merchants can become a loyalty program with technology, rules, and user experience without rebuilding from scratch each time.
